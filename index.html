Krystal Vasquez (she/her) is a science and environmental reporter who is currently wrapping up her PhD in atmospheric chemistry. Though she entered grad school with the intention of becoming a scientist, she quickly learned that writing about scientific studies was a lot more fun than conducting them.

Now, with the help of her research background, she is working to highlight Earth science research in ways that are fun and engaging for anyone, regardless of expertise. She is also passionate about covering environmental justice topics and advocating for increased inclusivity in STEM.

Her work has been published in several outlets, including Scientific American, Environmental Health News, Chemistry World, and Inside Science, among others. She also creates content for the American Chemical Society's ChemMatters Magazine and the I Am A Scientist project, and is an editorial assistant for Temblor Earthquake News.

Krystal is always on the lookout for new opportunities, whether it involves writing, editing or fact-checking. If you want to work with her, you can reach her at krystaltvasquez@gmail.com.

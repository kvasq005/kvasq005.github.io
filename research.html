Research

Though it may appear simple at a glance, our atmosphere is actually a complicated soup of compounds. The majority of these compounds fall under a class called oxygenated volatile organic compounds (OVOCs), which play key roles in the formation of air pollutants such as tropospheric ozone and secondary organic aerosols. Despite being such an important group of compounds, analytical challenges have hampered our ability to make ambient measurements of OVOCs. Therefore, my research has mainly revolved around developing different mass spectrometry methods so that we can more accurately measure different OVOCs in our atmosphere.

Using these new mass spectrometry methods, I can then use these data sets to explore the chemistry of specific OVOCs. In particular, I'm investigating whether an isomer-specific sink of these nitrates is responsible for discrepancies between field observations and model estimates of NOx, ozone and nitric acid.

Field Work

FIREX-AQ 2019
FIREX-AQ was a 2019 aircraft campaign jointly led by both NOAA and NASA. The goal of this campaign was to investigate the chemistry and transport of smoke from both wildfires and prescribed agricultural burns.

Campaign Website
Caltech Field Study 2017
I organized a small field study on the Caltech campus in Pasadena, CA. The aim of this study was to observe diurnal changes in the isomeric distributions of isoprene- and other alkene-derived oxidation products in a high NOx environment.

Data Repository
PROPHET-AMOS 2016
The 2016 PROPHET campaign brought together scientific collaborators with the goal of better understanding the sources, sinks and cycling of important radicals (HOx) and oxidation products in forested regions.

NASA SARP
The Student Airborne Research Program (SARP) is an eight-week summer internship program that allows students to acquire hands-on field experience by operating instruments upon one of the NASA Airborne Science Program flying science laboratories.
